The Amazon Rainforest is the largest tropical rainforest in the world, stretching across nine countries in South America, with most of it located in Brazil.

It is home to an incredible variety of plants and animals, many of which are found nowhere else on Earth.

It also provides food, water, and shelter for millions of people, including many Indigenous communities.
